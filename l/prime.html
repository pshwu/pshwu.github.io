Prime Telephone Numbers

Prime-counting function

\(\pi(x)\) denotes the number of prime numbers which are less or equal to \(x\). Phone numbers in United States has 10 digits, e.g. 919-123-4567. Thus the total number (or the upper bound) of prime phone numbers here should be (assuming phone number does not starts with "0"): $$\pi(10^{10})-\pi(10^9) = 455,052,511 - 50,847,534 = 404,204,977$$ This is 4.49% out of 9,000,000,000 possible phone numbers.
